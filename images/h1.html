What is Heart Disease?

Heart disease is a general term that refers to a variety of conditions that affect the heart's function. The most common form is coronary artery disease (CAD), which occurs when the blood vessels supplying the heart become narrow or blocked. This can cause chest pain, heart attacks, or even heart failure.

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) happens when the blood vessels that supply oxygen-rich blood to the heart muscle become blocked or narrowed by fatty deposits called plaque. This reduces the flow of oxygen to the heart, which can lead to chest pain (angina) or a heart attack.

Heart Attack occurs when the blood flow to part of the heart muscle is blocked, usually due to a clot. This blockage prevents oxygen from reaching the heart tissue, causing damage to the heart muscle. If not treated quickly, a heart attack can lead to serious complications or even death.

Arrhythmias are abnormal heart rhythms that can cause the heart to beat too fast, too slow, or irregularly. While some arrhythmias are harmless, others can be life-threatening and may require medical treatment or even surgery.

Heart Failure is a condition where the heart is unable to pump blood effectively. It can result from various factors, including coronary artery disease, high blood pressure, or heart attacks. Symptoms of heart failure include shortness of breath, fatigue, and swelling in the legs and feet.

Prevention and Treatment of heart disease often involves lifestyle changes such as adopting a heart-healthy diet, exercising regularly, quitting smoking, and managing stress. In some cases, medications or surgical interventions, such as stent placement or bypass surgery, may be needed to improve blood flow and prevent complications.
